Analysis of Weather Events impacting Health and Economy in USA

Yang Yuzhong

3/18/2015

Synopsis

This report outlines the analysis done to determine the impact of various weather events on the economy and health of United States. The data used in this report is based on the 1950 - 2011 storm database collected from U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration’s (NOAA).

Due to fewer events recorded for years earlier than 1996, the analysis was only performed on years between 1996-2011. The metrics for determining weather event impacts are Fatalities and Injuries for health impact and Property Damage and Crop Damage for economic impact.

The analysis results has shown that Tornado caused the highest number of casualties and Flood caused the highest economic damage to United States between 1996-2011. Also, Flood related events are highly ranked in terms of impact to United States’ health and economy.

Filtering the Dataset

The dataset is read in and all headers converted to lowercase with tolower() and names(). Next, strptime() and format() is performed on bgndate_ to extract the year for each entry in the dataset. year column is then added to the original dataset.

datecol<-strptime(data$bgn_date, "%m/%d/%Y %H:%M:%S", tz="UTC")
datecol<-format(datecol, "%Y")

data<-mutate(data,year=datecol)

Following this, datayear was created to hold the count of records for each year in the dataset.Based on information from Details of Storm Events, years earlier than 1996 were filtered from the dataset with %in% operator so that we perform analysis on years that have the most holistic types of weather events.

datayear<-data %>%
    group_by(year) %>%
    summarize(entries = length(evtype))

datayear<-datayear[datayear$year>1995,]

data<-filter(data,data$year %in% datayear$year)

Lastly, events without any fatalities, injuries, crop and property dmg are removed as they are not required to answer the questions for the analysis.

data<-filter(data,fatalities != 0 | injuries != 0 | propdmg != 0 | cropdmg != 0)

Determining health impact for each record

To determine health impact by event type, a new column totaldamage that is the sum of fatalities and injuries from event is added to the dataset using mutate().

data<-data %>%
    mutate(totalimpact=(data$fatalities + data$injuries))

Determining economic impact for each record

To determine economic impact by event type, the true value of crop damage, derived from cropdmg and cropdmgexp containing the exponential multplier. This is the same for property damage with propdmg and propdmgexp. Then the total economic impact is obtained by the sum of crop and property damage.

To do this, codebook from Investigative Reporters & Editors - Record Layout were used for the character symbols in cropdmgexp and propdmgexp, were used to convert them into numeric format.

data$cropdmgexp<-tolower(as.character(data$cropdmgexp))
data$cropdmgexp[data$cropdmgexp == "b"]<-"9"
data$cropdmgexp[data$cropdmgexp == "m"]<-"6"
data$cropdmgexp[data$cropdmgexp == "k"]<-"3"
data$cropdmgexp[data$cropdmgexp == "h"]<-"2"
data$cropdmgexp[data$cropdmgexp == ""]<-"0"
data$cropdmgexp[is.na(data$cropdmgexp)]<-"0"
data$cropdmgexp<-as.numeric(data$cropdmgexp)

data$propdmgexp<-tolower(as.character(data$propdmgexp))
data$propdmgexp[data$propdmgexp == "b"]<-"9"
data$propdmgexp[data$propdmgexp == "m"]<-"6"
data$propdmgexp[data$propdmgexp == "k"]<-"3"
data$propdmgexp[data$propdmgexp == "h"]<-"2"
data$propdmgexp[data$propdmgexp == ""]<-"0"
data$propdmgexp[is.na(data$propdmgexp)]<-"0"
data$propdmgexp<-as.numeric(data$propdmgexp)

After the conversion, a new column totaldamage that is the sum of crop and property damage is added to the dataset using mutate().

data<-data %>%
    mutate(totaldamage=(data$cropdmg * 10^data$cropdmgexp) + 
               (data$propdmg * 10^data$propdmgexp))

Merging Similar Event types

As the event types given in the dataset are too inconsistently named and manual merging of the event types into one of the 48 event types reflected in Storm Data Documentation would be too tedious, a partial merging of similar event types that have high health and economic impact is performed.

Firstly, to determine the event types to merge, healthimpact and economicimpact are created by summing up totalimpact and totaldamage columns respectively then arranged in descending order. This helps in determining the top 20 high health and economic impact event types to perform merging to reduce the scope of merging similar event types.

tophealth<- data %>%

After the merging of similar event types, healthimpact and economicimpact is created by summing of totalimpact and totaldamage columns respectively then arranged in descending order.

healthimpact<- data %>%
    group_by(evtype) %>%
    summarize(totalimpact=sum(totalimpact)) %>%
    arrange(desc(totalimpact))

economicimpact<-data %>%
    group_by(evtype) %>%
    summarize(totaldamage=sum(totaldamage)/10^6) %>%
    arrange(desc(totaldamage))

Results

After preprocessing the data, analysis are performed to determine weather events types that are most harmful w.r.t Population Health and had the greatest economic impact.

Events Most Harmful with Respect to Population Health

healthdata<-healthimpact[1:10,]
healthdata$evtype<-factor(healthdata$evtype,
                          level = as.character(healthdata$evtype))
healthplot <- ggplot(data = healthdata, aes(x = evtype, y = totalimpact)) +
    theme(axis.text.x = element_text(angle = 60, hjust = 1)) +
    geom_bar(stat="identity") +
    ggtitle("Most Harmful Weather Event Types in USA (1996-2011)") +
    xlab("Weather Event Type") +
    ylab("No. of Casualties")
healthplot

From the health impact barplot, Tornado caused the highest number of casualties between 1996-2011. This is almost double that of Flood and Heat related weather events.

Events with Greatest Economic Consequences

econdata<-economicimpact[1:10,]
econdata$evtype<-factor(econdata$evtype, 
                        level = as.character(econdata$evtype))
econplot<- ggplot(data = econdata, aes(x = evtype, y = totaldamage)) +

    ggtitle("Costliest Weather Event Types in USA (1996-2011)") +
    xlab("Weather Event Type") +
    ylab("Property/Crop Damage (in $USD Millions)")
econplot

From the economic impact barplot, Flood caused the largest Property and Crop related damage. This is almost triple that of Hurricane and Storm Surge related weather events.

Conclusion

Flood related weather events rank highly in terms of economic impact and number of casualty. Tornado is 4th in terms of property/crop damage although it causes the highest number of casualties.
